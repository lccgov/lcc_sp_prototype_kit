2. Discounts and exemptions for empty properties

In the following circumstances, you may not have to pay council tax, or you may be able to get a discount or exemption:

  • Click to expand Care homes, hospitals or hostels

    If a person who normally lives at an address is now living in a residential care home, hospital or certain type of hostel and is not intending to return home, we may be able to grant a council tax discount. If nobody else lives at the address, we may even be able to grant an exemption, so there will be no council tax to pay at all.

    Please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Care homes, hospitals or hostels claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Moving to another address to be cared for

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have had to move out to be cared for somewhere else (but not in a nursing home or care home), we may be able to grant an exemption for the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Receiving care claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Moving to another address to care for someone

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have left to care for someone, we may be able to grant an exemption to the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Providing care claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Last occupier has died

    There are certain circumstances where we may be able to grant an exemption from council tax if the liable person has died. Further information can be found in our guidance document.

    If you think an exemption may apply, contact us. We will need to know what your relationship is to the person who has died. If emailing please include your phone number so we can use it to contact you if necessary.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

    • Property unoccupied after a death guidance document(PDF, 60KB)
  • Click to expand Property unoccupied and unfurnished for seven days or less

    Please contact us with your name, address, council tax account number and an explanation of the circumstances. Your account number can be found on your bill.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

    Post: Leeds City Council, P.O. Box 60, LS2 8JR

  • Click to expand In prison or otherwise detained

    You may be able to get a discount or exemption if a person who normally lives at your address is:

    • on remand, or in prison for a crime other than non-payment of council tax
    • being held in a hospital or other place on mental health grounds as a result of a court order
    • being held under immigration law

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Prisoner or person detained discount claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Repossessed property

    Please provide proof of your eligibility along with your name, address and council tax account number - your account number can be found on your bill. You can take your proof to a One Stop Centre or post it to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

  • Click to expand Annexes

    If a single property contains two or more dwellings, the ‘annexe’ may qualify for a 50% discount if either:

    • it is occupied by a relative of the person liable for the main dwelling
    • it is being used (but not lived in) by a resident of the main dwelling – this dwelling must be their sole or main residence

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Annexe discount claim form(PDF, 50KB)

    Exemptions for annexes

    An exemption will apply if an annexe is unoccupied and cannot be let separately because of planning conditions.

    If an exemption is granted no council tax will be payable for the annexe. Please contact us to discuss your situation.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

  • Click to expand Occupation prohibited by law

    This exemption can be claimed if a property has been deemed unsafe to live in, for example the property contains asbestos and has been deemed unfit to live in by Environmental Health.

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

  • Click to expand Trustee responsible for a property following bankruptcy

    Please provide us with the details of the trustee, the name of the court that granted the bankruptcy order and the date this was granted. We will also need your name, address, and council tax account number - your account number can be found on your bill.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

    Post: Leeds City Council, P.O. Box 60, LS2 8JR

  • Click to expand Unoccupied property left empty by a student

    A property left unoccupied because the person responsible for council tax is a student who is living elsewhere for study purposes may be eligible for an exemption.

    When last occupied, the property must have been the sole or main residence of a person who is a student.

    The student must have begun living elsewhere for study purposes within six weeks of leaving the property.

    The course must be a full-time course and last for at least one academic year, take more than 24 weeks in that year and involve more than 21 hours a week of study.

    If you wish to apply, please contact us:

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

  • Click to expand Empty caravan pitch or mooring

    You will need to provide us with your name, address, and council tax account number - your account number can be found on your bill.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

    Post: Leeds City Council, P.O. Box 60, LS2 8JR

  • Click to expand Unoccupied property owned and last occupied by a registered charity

    Please contact us to discuss this exemption further.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

  • Click to expand Unoccupied property for use by a minister of religion

    Please contact us to discuss this exemption further.

    Phone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

Tell us about a change

If your circumstances change and you no longer qualify for a discount or exemption you must let us know.

You’ll need to provide us with:

  • the reason you need to cancel
  • your council tax account number
  • your contact number
  • the date you want to cancel your discount or exemption from
