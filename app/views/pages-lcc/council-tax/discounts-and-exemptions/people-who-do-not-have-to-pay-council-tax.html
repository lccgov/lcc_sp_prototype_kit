Find out who is exempt and how this can affect how much council tax is paid.

How the number of people living in a property affects the council tax bill

The council tax charge is based upon the number of people living in a property. If someone is exempt, it means that we don't count them when we work out the number of people living there.

  • If all the people living in a property are exempt, no council tax needs to be paid
  • If all the people living in a property are exempt apart from one, the liable person may be able to get a reduction on their bill
  • If there is more than one person in the property who isn’t exempt, no reduction will be given

Who doesn’t have to pay?

These people are exempt from paying council tax:

  • full time students, school and college leavers aged 18 and 19
  • student nurses
  • a dependant non-British spouse of a student
  • some young people, youth training trainees and apprentices
  • unpaid carers or care workers
  • people who have a severe mental impairment
  • people in prison, on remand or being held in a hospital
  • members of religious communities (monks and nuns)
  • members of family living in an annexe
  • members of visiting forces and dependants of international headquarters and defence organisations – contact us

Tell us about a change

If your circumstances change and you no longer qualify for a reduction or exemption you must let us know by telephone:

0113 222 4404

or email:

council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

You will need to provide us with:

  • the reason you need to cancel
  • your council tax account number
  • your contact number
  • the date you want to cancel your reduction or exemption from
