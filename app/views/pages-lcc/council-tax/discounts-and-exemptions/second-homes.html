Find out whether you can claim a discount when you own or spend time in a second home

If you own or spend time at more than one property, a discount can only be applied to the property that we consider to be your main residence.

Examples of the things we take into account are:

  • whether you physically reside at each address
  • the reasons why you have two addresses
  • where your family lives, if you have one
  • where you keep most of your belongings
  • whether you intend to return to one address eventually

Job-related second homes

You may be able to claim a discount if you occupy a second home because of your job.

One of the following things must apply to the job-related property:

  • the duties of the job make it necessary for you to live in that particular property
  • you can carry out your job better by living in that particular property, and it is usual for employers to provide accommodation in that type of job
  • your contract requires you to live in that particular property

Empty second homes

The same rules apply to second homes as they do to your main residence.
