Council tax charges on properties that are unoccupied, unfurnished or undergoing major works.

Properties that can be lived in

If you own a property that is empty but can be lived in, it’s likely that you will need to pay full council tax. However, in certain circumstances, you may not have to pay council tax.

If any of the following apply, please complete and return the relevant form:

  • the liable person is staying long-term in a care home, hostel or hospital
  • the liable person has moved to another address to be cared for
  • the liable person has moved to another address to care for someone
  • the liable person is in prison
  • the liable person has died and certain conditions are met

If any of the following apply, please provide proof of your eligibility (if needed) along with your name, address, and council tax account number (this can be found on your bill). You can take your proof to a One Stop Centre or Community Hub or post it to:

Leeds City Council,
PO BOX 60,
Leeds
LS2 8JR

  • the property has been unoccupied and unfurnished for 7 days or less (no proof required)
  • the mortgage lender has repossessed it
  • the property is being kept vacant for a religious minister to move into
  • the property is the responsibility of a trustee in bankruptcy
  • you have an empty caravan pitch or mooring

Properties that can’t be lived in

If your property is uninhabitable, you will need to contact the Valuation Office Agency to have your property removed from banding, which means no council tax will need to be paid.

What is considered uninhabitable

Examples include:

  • roof work that's needed on safety grounds
  • removal of supporting walls or floor joists
  • subsidence
  • severe dry rot requiring extensive treatments or remedies

What is not considered uninhabitable

Examples include:

  • replacement kitchen or bathroom
  • replacement windows
  • general refurbishment
  • re-wiring, re-plumbing, plastering

Empty homes charge

You will pay an extra 50% in addition to your normal council tax bill if your property has been empty for two years or more. This does not apply if your property is exempt from council tax or if the Valuation Office Agency has removed it from banding.

Please note that the two year period is calculated from when the property first became empty and unfurnished.

If you have an empty property that you want to bring back into use, contact the Empty Property Team.
