1. Overview

Council tax charges on properties that are unoccupied or uninhabitable.

Furnished properties

There is no discount for a furnished unoccupied property so full council tax is payable for any unoccupied period, unless certain criteria are met.

Unfurnished properties

You won’t have to pay council tax if a property is unoccupied and unfurnished for seven days or less.

If the property is empty for more than seven days there is no discount. This means that full council tax is payable from the date it became empty.

You will pay an extra 50% if your property has been unoccupied and unfurnished for two years or more. The two year period starts from when the property first became empty and unfurnished, which means if you have recently bought a property which has been empty for a long time, you might have to pay the additional 50%. This is known as the “long-term empty premium”.

Long-term empty properties

If you have an empty property and you would like advice about how to bring it back into use, contact the Empty Property Team.

Landlords and tenants: who pays the council tax?

The owner is liable for the council tax bill whilst the property is empty. However, in the following circumstances, the tenant remains liable for paying the bill:

  • if they have a fixed –term tenancy agreement for six months or more and that tenancy is still in force, or
  • if they are ‘contractual periodic tenants’ whose tenancy has not officially ended. This means they are on a ‘rolling contract’ but their original tenancy (for six months or more) contained wording which indicates that the original tenancy continues after the end of the fixed term period. In this case, the tenant will remain liable until the end of the agreed period of notice

Once a new tenant moves in, they will become liable for the council tax bill.

Properties that are uninhabitable

If you think your property is uninhabitable, contact the Valuation Office Agency. They will decide if the property can be classed as uninhabitable and whether any council tax needs to be paid.
