Council tax charges on properties that are unoccupied, unfurnished or uninhabitable

Properties that can be lived in

If you own a property that is empty but can be lived in, itâ€™s likely that you will need to pay full council tax. However, in the following circumstances, you may not have to pay council tax, or you may be able to get a discount:

  • Click to expand The liable person is staying long-term in a care home, hostel or hospital

    If a person who normally lives at an address is now living in a residential care home, hospital or certain type of hostel and is not intending to return home, we may be able to grant a council tax discount. If nobody else lives at the address, we may even be able to grant an exemption, so there will be no council tax to pay at all.

    Please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Revenues Service
    PO BOX 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Care home resident claim form(PDF, 27Kb)
  • Click to expand The liable person has moved to another address to be cared for

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have had to move out to be cared for somewhere else (but not in a nursing home or care home), we may be able to grant an exemption for the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Revenues Service
    PO BOX 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Exemption claim form(PDF, 27Kb)
  • Click to expand The liable person has moved to another address to care for someone

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have left to care for someone, we may be able to grant an exemption to the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Revenues Service
    PO BOX 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Exemption providing care claim form(PDF, 27Kb)
  • Click to expand The liable person is in prison

    You may be able to get a discount if a person who normally lives at your address is:

    • on remand, or in prison for a crime other than non-payment of council tax
    • being held in a hospital or other place on mental health grounds as a result of a court order
    • being held under immigration law

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Revenues Service
    PO BOX 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Prisoner discount claim form(PDF, 27Kb)
  • Click to expand The liable person has moved to another address to care for someone

    There are certain circumstances where we may be able to grant an exemption from council tax if the liable person has died (PDF, 11Kb).

    If you think an exemption may apply, contact us. We will need to know what your relationship is to the person who has died. If emailing please include your phone number so we can use it to contact you if necessary.

Properties that are uninhabitable

You will pay an extra 50% in addition to your normal council tax bill if your property has been empty for two years or more. This does not apply if your property is exempt from council tax or if the Valuation Office Agency says no council tax needs to be paid.

Please note that the two year period is calculated from when the property first became empty and unfurnished.

Long-term empty property

If you have an empty property and you would like advice about how to bring it back into use, contact the Empty Property Team.
