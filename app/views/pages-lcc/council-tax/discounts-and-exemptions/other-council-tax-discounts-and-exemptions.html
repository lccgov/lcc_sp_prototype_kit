Other council tax discounts or exemptions that you may be eligible for.

  • Click to expand Student nurses

    You may qualify for a council tax discount if you are either:

    • employed as a student nurse but taking a course which will lead to first registration in the Nurses’ Part or the Midwives’ Part of the register maintained by the Nursing and Midwifery Council under the Nursing and Midwifery Order 2001
    • not employed as a nurse but taking a nursing degree or diploma course and getting a student grant or scholarship

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Student nurse discount application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Student nurse discount application form(PDF, 47KB)
  • Click to expand Young people

    A council tax discount may apply for a young person if either:

    • they are aged between 18 and 19 and enrolled on a further education course for more than three months, and studying for more than twelve hours per week; or
    • their parent or guardian is entitled to claim child benefit for them (or would be if the young person was not in local authority care)

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Young person discount appliction form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Young person discount appliction form(PDF, 29KB)
  • Click to expand Apprentices

    You may qualify for a council tax discount if you meet the following criteria:

    • you won’t be paid more than £195 a week
    • the training leads to a qualification accredited by a body recognised by the Office of Qualifications and Examinations Regulation (Ofqual) or the Scottish Vocational Education Council (SVEC)

    To apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Apprentice discount application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Apprentice discount appliction form(PDF, 30KB)
  • Click to expand Unpaid carers

    You may be able to claim a council tax discount if you meet the following criteria:

    • you are living with and caring for someone who has a disability and is entitled to certain benefits. This person must not be your spouse, partner or child under 18
    • you are providing care for at least 35 hours a week on average

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Unpaid carer's discount application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Unpaid carer's discount application form(PDF, 34KB)
  • Click to expand Care workers

    Care workers may be able to claim a council tax discount if they meet the following criteria:

    • employed as a carer by an organisation such as Community Service Volunteers, by a public body or by the person he/she is caring for (if introduced to that person by a charity);
    • living in accommodation provided by the employer so that the care can be given;
    • employed to provide care for at least 24 hours a week and paid no more than £44 a week

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Carer discount application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Carer discount application form(PDF, 30KB)
  • Click to expand Severe mental impairment

    A discount can be claimed for people who have a severe mental impairment if they meet the following criteria:

    • the person must have a severe impairment of intelligence and social functioning which appears to be permanent, as confirmed by a registered medical practitioner (such as their doctor)
    • they must be entitled to certain benefits, or would be entitled if they were not of pensionable age, or if their partner was not getting a premium in their Job Seeker’s Allowance for them

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Severe mental impairment claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Severe mental impairment claim form(PDF, 87KB)
  • Click to expand In prison, on remand or being held in a hospital

    You may be able to get a discount or exemption if a person who normally lives at your address is:

    • on remand, or in prison for a crime other than non-payment of council tax
    • being held in a hospital or other place on mental health grounds as a result of a court order
    • being held under immigration law

    For further information and to apply for the discount or exemption, please download, complete and return the Prison/person detained discount application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Prison/person detained discount application form(PDF, 37KB)
  • Click to expand Religious communities

    If you are part of a religious community (for example a convent or a monastery) and are dependant on that community for your material needs, you may be eligible for an exemption.

    To apply, please download, complete and return the Religious communities disregard application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Religious communities disregard application form(PDF, 86KB)
  • Click to expand Annexes

    If a single property contains two or more dwellings, the ‘annexe’ may qualify for a 50% discount if:

    • it is occupied by a relative of the person liable for the main dwelling, or
    • it is being used (but not lived in) by a resident of the main dwelling – this dwelling must be their sole or main residence

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the Annexe discount claim application form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Annexe discount claim application form(PDF, 50KB)

    If an annexe cannot be let separately because of planning conditions and is either unoccupied, or occupied by a dependant relative (aged 65 or over, or severely mentally impaired, or substantially and permanently disabled), an exemption may apply. If an exemption is granted, no council tax will be payable for the annexe.

  • Click to expand Care homes, hostels and hospitals

    If a person who normally lives at an address is now living in a residential care home, hospital or certain type of hostel and is not intending to return home, we may be able to grant a council tax discount. If nobody else lives at the address, we may even be able to grant an exemption, so there will be no council tax to pay at all.

    Please download, complete and return the Home/Hospital/Hostel resident discount or exemption claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Home/Hospital/Hostel Resident discount or exemption claim form(PDF, 125KB)
  • Click to expand Moving to another address to be cared for

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have had to move out to be cared for somewhere else (but not in a nursing home or care home), we may be able to grant an exemption to the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the Exemption I: Property left empty by people receiving care form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Exemption I: Property left empty by people receiving care form(PDF, 113KB)
  • Click to expand Moving to another address to care for someone

    If somebody owns, or is the tenant of a property which is unoccupied because they have had to care for someone, we may be able to grant an exemption to the unoccupied property. This means that there will be no council tax to pay.

    For further information and to apply for the exemption, please download, complete and return the Exemption J: Property left empty by people providing care form to:

    Leeds City Council
    PO box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Exemption J: Property left empty by people providing care form(PDF, 67KB)
  • Click to expand Last occupier has died

    There are certain circumstances where we may be able to grant an exemption from council tax if the liable person has died. Further information can be found in our guidance document.

    If you think an exemption may apply, contact us. We will need to know what your relationship is to the person who has died. If emailing please include your phone number so we can use it to contact you if necessary.

    Telephone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)
    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk
    • Property unoccupied after a death guidance document(PDF, 60KB)
  • Click to expand Members and dependents of visiting forces, international headquarters and defence organisations

    We may be able to grant a council tax discount where an occupant is a member or non-British dependant of a visiting force, international headquarters or defence organisation.

    If you think this may apply, contact us.

    Telephone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)
    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

Tell us about a change

If your circumstances change and you no longer qualify for a discount you must let us know. You will need to provide us with:

  • the reason you need to cancel
  • your council tax account number
  • your contact number
  • the date you want to cancel your discount or exemption from
Telephone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)
Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

Further help

For further help or advice you can contact us or visit a one stop centre.

Telephone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)
Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk
