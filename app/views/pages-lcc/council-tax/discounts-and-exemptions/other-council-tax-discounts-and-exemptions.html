Other council tax discounts or exemptions that you may be eligible for.

  • Click to expand Young people

    A council tax discount may apply for a young person over 18 if either:

    • they are aged between 18 and 19 and enrolled on a further education course for more than three months, and studying for more than twelve hours per week; or
    • their parent or guardian is entitled to claim Child Benefit for them (or would be if the young person was not in local authority care)

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Young person discount claim form(PDF, 29KB)
  • Click to expand Severe mental impairment

    A discount can be claimed for people who have a severe mental impairment if they meet all the following criteria:

    • the person must have a severe impairment of intelligence and social functioning which appears to be permanent, as confirmed by a registered medical practitioner (such as their doctor)
    • they must be entitled to certain benefits, or would be entitled if they were not of pensionable age, or if their partner was not getting a premium in their Job Seeker’s Allowance for them

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Severe mental impairment claim form(PDF, 87KB)
  • Click to expand Unpaid carers

    You may be able to claim a council tax discount if you meet the following criteria:

    • you are living with and caring for someone who has a disability and is entitled to certain benefits. This person must not be your spouse, partner or child under 18
    • you are providing care for at least 35 hours a week on average

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Unpaid carer's discount claim form(PDF, 34KB)
  • Click to expand Care workers

    Care workers may be able to claim a council tax discount if they meet all the following criteria:

    • employed as a carer by an organisation, public body or by the person he/she is caring for (if introduced to that person by a charity)
    • living in accommodation provided by the employer so that the care can be given
    • employed to provide care for at least 24 hours a week and paid no more than £44 a week

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Carer discount claim form(PDF, 30KB)
  • Click to expand Care homes, hospitals or hostels

    If a person who normally lives at an address is now living in a residential care home, hospital or certain type of hostel and is not intending to return home, we may be able to grant a council tax discount. If nobody else lives at the address, we may even be able to grant an exemption, so there will be no council tax to pay at all.

    Please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Care homes, hospitals or hostels claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Apprentices

    You may qualify for a council tax discount if you meet the following criteria:

    • you won’t be paid more than £195 a week
    • the training leads to a qualification accredited by a body recognised by the Office of Qualifications and Examinations Regulation (Ofqual) or the Scottish Vocational Education Council (SVEC)

    To apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Apprentice discount claim form(PDF, 30KB)
  • Click to expand Student nurses

    You may qualify for a council tax discount if you are either:

    • employed as a student nurse and taking a course which will lead to registration in the Nurses’ or Midwives’ section of the register maintained by the Nursing and Midwifery Council under the Nursing and Midwifery Order 2001
    • not employed as a nurse but taking a nursing degree or diploma course and getting a student grant or scholarship

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Student nurse discount claim form(PDF, 47KB)
  • Click to expand In prison or otherwise detained

    You may be able to get a discount or exemption if a person who normally lives at your address is:

    • on remand, or in prison for a crime other than non-payment of council tax
    • being held in a hospital or other place on mental health grounds as a result of a court order
    • being held under immigration law

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Prisoner or person detained discount claim form(PDF, 27KB)
  • Click to expand Annexes

    If a single property contains two or more dwellings, the ‘annexe’ may qualify for a 50% discount if either:

    • it is occupied by a relative of the person liable for the main dwelling
    • it is being used (but not lived in) by a resident of the main dwelling – this dwelling must be their sole or main residence

    For further information and to apply for the discount, please download, complete and return the claim form to:

    Leeds City Council
    P.O. Box 60
    LS2 8JR

    • Annexe discount claim form(PDF, 50KB)

    Exemptions for annexes

    An exemption will apply if an annexe is either:

    • occupied by a dependant relative (aged 65 or over, or severely mentally impaired, or substantially and permanently disabled)
    • unoccupied and cannot be let separately because of planning conditions

    If an exemption is granted no council tax will be payable for the annexe. Please contact us to discuss your situation.

    Telephone: 0113 222 4404 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    Email: council.tax@leeds.gov.uk

Tell us about a change

If your circumstances change and you no longer qualify for a discount you must let us know. You will need to provide us with:

  • your council tax account number - your account number can be found on your bill
  • your contact number
  • the reason you need to cancel
  • the date you want to cancel your discount or exemption from
