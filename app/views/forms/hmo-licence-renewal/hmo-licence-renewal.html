What is a licensable HMO

If you are a private landlord/managing agent and let out a property as a licensable HMO, that property must have a valid licence issued by the council. A licensable HMO is one where the property:

  • Comprises 3 or more storeys;
  • Has 5 or more unrelated occupiers (consisting of 2 or more households) and;
  • Who share an amenity such as a kitchen, toilet, bathroom or lounge.

If the property consists of a mixture of self-contained flats and flats where the tenant has to go out of their flat usually through a communal area to make use of an amenity as described above (whether for their exclusive use or not), then this may require a HMO licence. In addition, properties let above commercial premises may require a HMO licence.

    This form can ONLY be used in the following circumstances:

    • By an existing licence holder wishing to renew an UNEXPIRED licence; AND
    • Where there are no material changes at the property and you are able to make a statutory declaration to that effect If you are wanting to add or change the licence holder/occupancy numbers or your licence has expired, you must use the full HMO licence application form
  • Click to expandWhat information do I need to fill out a renewal form?

    You must have the following:

    • Existing licence reference number of the property you wish to renew;
    • Fit and proper person number;
    • Leeds Rental Standard membership number;
    • A means of paying the licence fee on-line. Please email us HMO.Team@leeds.gov.uk if you do not have this information. We will verify your details and provide you with this.
  • Click to expandHow much will my licence cost?

    HMO Licence Fees:

    Property Type HMO Licence Fee (Accredited Landlord) HMO Licence Fee (Non-accredited Landlord)
    5 - 6 occupants £675 £825
    7 - 8 occupants £850 £1,000
    9 + occupants £1,200 £1,350
  • Click to expandPublic register

    The council is required by law to establish and maintain a register of all HMO licences granted. As such your name and address (as it appears on the licence) will appear on this register and will be made available for inspection by members of the public at all reasonable times. A shorter version of the register can be viewed on-line at Data Mill North website

  • Click to expandIncorrect or misleading information & licence application withdrawals

    If we become aware of any information which is found to be incorrect or misleading with regards to discount eligibility we will seek to recover the value of the discount claimed. The council reserves the right to issue a shorter duration HMO licence if there is reason to believe that an applicant has previously evaded HMO licensing or has provided incorrect or misleading information for the purpose of obtaining a licence discount. In such cases the applicant will be given the opportunity to provide evidence to the contrary. Accreditation discounts will not be available to landlords where it appears that a timely application has not been made to the council. If after applying and paying for your renewal you decide to withdraw your licence application part way through the administrative process, the council, upon written request by the applicant giving reasons for its withdrawal, will issue a refund minus an administration fee of £150.
